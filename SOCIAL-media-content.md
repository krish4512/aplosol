## POST #6: LinkedIn - Personal Story

**Title:** "Why I Built Aplosol (And Why It Matters)"

**Content:**

Five years ago, I was teaching basic tech skills to high school students. One day, a kid asked:

"What's the difference between AI and a regular computer?"

I tried to explain. Talked about algorithms, training data, neural networks...

His eyes glazed over.

That night, I thought: "He's going to graduate not understanding one of the most important technologies of his lifetime."

It wasn't his fault. We just don't have good ways to teach this stuff to kids.

**Fast forward to 2026:**

I have a 8-year-old nephew. Same question. So I decided to answer it *properly*.

I spent months building a simple, honest curriculum. No jargon. No coding. Just: "Here's what AI is, here's how it works, here's what it can't do."

45 minutes total.

He got it.

His parents got it.

His teacher asked if she could use it in class.

So I refined it. Tested with 50 kids. Same result: High engagement, clear understanding, kids excited about the future (instead of scared).

**That became Aplosol.**

I'm not trying to train the next generation of AI engineers. There are platforms for that.

I'm trying to give every kid a foundation. So they're not confused when AI shows up in their job, their school, their life.

**That's the mission.**

If you're an educator, school leader, or parent: I'd love your feedback. What would make this better?
